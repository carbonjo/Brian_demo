\section{Introduction}

A \textbf{Riemann sum} approximates the definite integral \(\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\) by dividing the interval \([a,b]\) into \(n\) subintervals and summing the areas of rectangles whose heights are given by the function \(f\).

\section{Setup}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Partition: \(a = x_0 < x_1 < \cdots < x_n = b\)
  \item Subinterval width: \(\Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n}\) (equal subintervals)
  \item Sample point in \([x_{i-1}, x_i]\): \(x_i^*\)
\end{itemize}

\section{Definition}

The Riemann sum is
\[
  S_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i^*)\, \Delta x.
\]

\section{Common choices for \(x_i^*\)}

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \begin{tabular}{ll}
    \toprule
    Type & \(x_i^*\) \\
    \midrule
    Left   & \(x_{i-1}\) \\
    Right  & \(x_i\) \\
    Midpoint & \(\frac{x_{i-1}+x_i}{2}\) \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\section{Example}

Let \(f(x)=x^2\) on \([0,2]\) with \(n=4\), \(\Delta x = \frac{1}{2}\).

\textbf{Left sum:}
\[
  S_4^{\text{left}} = f(0)\cdot\frac{1}{2} + f(0.5)\cdot\frac{1}{2} + f(1)\cdot\frac{1}{2} + f(1.5)\cdot\frac{1}{2}
  = 0 + 0.125 + 0.5 + 1.125 = 1.75.
\]

The exact integral is \(\int_0^2 x^2\,dx = \frac{8}{3} \approx 2.667\). As \(n\to\infty\), \(S_n \to \int_a^b f(x)\,dx\).

\section{Limit definition of the integral}

\[
  \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i^*)\, \Delta x.
\]
